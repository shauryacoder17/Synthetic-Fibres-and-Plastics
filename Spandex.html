Spandex



Spandex- Spandex, Lycra, or elastane is a synthetic fiber known for its exceptional elasticity. It was invented by the DuPont chemist Joseph Shivers in 1959.
The generic name "spandex", is an anagram of the word "expands".



Benefits of Spandex:
• It allows for easy, quick, and uniform dye application.
• It has excellent elasticity, which makes it suitable for use in clothes that require snug fittings.
• It has soft hand feeling, best elastic, comfortable wearing, and perfect fit
• Spandex is acid and alkaline resistant. It is also resistant to perspiration, sea water, and also resistant to kinds of dry cleaner and most of suntan lotion.
• Spandex has good dye ability, and resists fading.

Uses of Spandex:
• It finds applications as oil seals, footwear soles and heels, construction of conveyor belts, printing rollers, fabric coatings, water-proof items, tank-lining in some chemical plants, cable sheathing, pump impellers, gaskets and diaphragms, industrial hoses etc..
• Heat setting treatment is used for ensuring dimensional stability of fabrics and garments.
• It can also be used in sports textiles like swimwear, cycling jersey, apparel for exercise and in apparel like leggings, shorts, skinny jeans, ski pants, yoga pants, hosiery, surgical hose, etc.
